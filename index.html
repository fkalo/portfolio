How do I do it?

let me show you my methods

You must've heard the term
  • 'Design Thinking'
  • 'Double Diamond'
– here's how I applied them in the past few years.
discover
  • Let's face it, we can never do enough discovery. That's why I use our product's community portal, where I can observe, listen and engage in conversation with our users directly.
  • Besides, to explore the user needs and collect problem-specific data, I usually conduct interviews.
  • Although we lacked of this opportunity due to COVID, I think the most important research is observation, especially with architects.
define
  • After collecting “all” relevant data, we get deeper and try to make some sense out of them by the help of affinity sorting and empathy mapping.
  • Despite the fact that this phase is not about research - by the book at least -, I often realise that we need some more info to fill in some gaps. That's when I turn to the users once more, like detective Columbo with just one more thing.
  • Also user personas for our products vary a lot. Using them during journey mapping and story mapping is crucial.
develop
  • Like research, story mapping and user journey mapping doesn't stop after one phase either.
  • Apart from ideating with the team(s) and fellow designers and quickly visualising these through prototypes, our complex product needs to be evaluated too. To map connecting areas and gather all necessary information for consistency and feasibility.
  • Remember Columbo? I would give a call or write to our interviewees with just one more thing, to quickly validate the proposed concept.
deliver
  • Deliver phase starts when the question “what” is replaced by “how”.
  • Apart from having smaller tasks, such as drawing the last icons, finalising copy and being prepared to answer questions of QA Engineers, I always make time to check the finished stories and sometimes we iterate a little on the design or implementation.
  • But! “A design isn't finished until someone is using it.” - Our enterprise product has an alpha and beta release period, which allows us to connect with our users and evaluate our previous decisions.
