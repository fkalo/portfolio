Designflows 2021
Designflows is a UI competition organised and sponsored by Bending Spoons. Around 1200 contestants compete each year. I also took my chances in 2021 and here's how it turned out.
The assignment

“Your mission, should you choose to accept it, is to design a range of materials for Homely - a smartphone app for people who need to do some simple Do It Yourself (DIY) tasks.”

Contestants had to design the following materials for the Homely smartphone app:

  • App Icon. We had to stick to the shape of icons used in the current version of iOS. We were designing an icon for iOS only — Android was excluded from this element of the task.
  • Toolkit Rental screen. The only must-have was a search bar — the rest was up to us.
  • My Rentals screen. This is where users manage and track their past, present, and future bookings.
  • How it works screen. Users see this before or after the payment screen. Possible elements to include are instructions on how to get the tools from the locker, safety instructions, information about late return fees and tool damage fees, and more. The choice again, was ours.

I took two important research findings into consideration during my design:

  • People usually are either particularly beginners or
  • They are experienced DIY-ers, who happen to lack of some uncommon tools for their next project.

Therefore I wanted to give them both the opportunity to easily find what they're looking for. So I included different call-to-action items on the Toolkit Rental screen to ensure just that.

The other small research - I carried out on every single person I talked to in 48 hours - was focusing on the visualization of the application.

One consistent feedback was about the associated colour scheme. A good 80% of people associated some kind of yellow colour with the topic, which was interesting. So of course, I included the yellowish colour in my design.

Shapes, arrangements and proportions evolved simultaneously between the screens and the application logo.

My takeaways

The available timeframe for the whole task was one weekend, we received the task on a Friday evening, and needed to submit our work on the following Monday morning.

The most important lesson I've learnt was the fact that I really am able to achieve something within a couple of days.

Also looking back on my design after a while is pretty interesting and educational: I realized, this job was perfect for a first round, but I would definitely iterate a few more times with my designs in the future.
